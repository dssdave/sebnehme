Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions people actually ask Seb before booking a call.

Is Grand Cayman the same as the Cayman Islands?

Not quite. The Cayman Islands are three islands — Grand Cayman, Cayman Brac, and Little Cayman. Grand Cayman is the largest and by far the most populated, home to George Town, Seven Mile Beach, and nearly all the financial services activity. When people say "Cayman" in a relocation or business context, they almost always mean Grand Cayman specifically.

What's the Cayman Islands government like for someone moving here?

Cayman is a British Overseas Territory with its own elected government — a Premier and Legislative Assembly handling local affairs — while the UK retains responsibility for defense, foreign policy, and internal security through a Governor. For a relocating founder, the practical upshot is a stable common-law legal system, no local income tax legislation to navigate, and immigration rules set and enforced by Cayman's own department, not London.

Do I really pay zero income tax if I move to the Cayman Islands?

On Cayman-sourced income, yes — there's no personal income tax, capital gains tax, or corporate tax charged by the Cayman government. What you still owe depends entirely on your home country. Americans, for instance, are taxed on worldwide income no matter where they live. See our tax guide for the country-by-country breakdown.
